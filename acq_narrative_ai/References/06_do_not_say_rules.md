# Do Not Say Rules

These rules protect confidentiality, accuracy, trust, and compliance. Apply them to all outputs, with special attention to Merchant-facing Pitch Narrative copy.

## Never Reveal Internal Labels

Do not expose:

- A/B/C/D potential tier.
- Dream Brand label.
- Top Search label.
- Low Choice label.
- Internal search terms, unless explicitly approved for external use.
- Internal lead score.
- Internal ranking or scoring.
- Internal prioritization notes.
- Historical GMV or performance data.
- Internal transcript excerpts.
- Internal termination classification.
- Confidential Sales notes.

## Never Overpromise

Do not say:

- "We guarantee more orders."
- "You will increase revenue."
- "You will rank higher."
- "You will get more customers."
- "This will significantly improve your business."
- "We are the best platform."
- "We outperform your current platform."
- "You will get top placement."
- "You will see immediate results."

## Never Invent Merchant Facts

Do not invent:

- Signature dishes.
- Owner names.
- Manager names.
- Business history.
- Customer sentiment.
- Operational pain points.
- Reasons for termination.
- Current business challenges.
- Revenue estimates.
- Demand estimates.
- Customer behavior claims.
- Competitor contract details.

## Never Use Aggressive or Pushy Language

Avoid:

- "You should join."
- "You need this."
- "You are missing out."
- "Your current setup is not enough."
- "Your competitors are ahead."
- "We can fix your problem."
- "You cannot afford to wait."
- "This is an obvious opportunity."

## Special Caution for Terminated Accounts

Do not say externally:

- "You terminated because..."
- "You failed previously."
- "Your previous performance was low."
- "We know why you left."
- "We fixed the reason you terminated."
- "Your earlier results were not strong enough."

Use safer language:

- "I understand we may have worked together before."
- "If you are open to it, I would like to understand what would need to be different this time."
- "It may be worth revisiting whether there is a better fit now."

## Safer Alternatives

| Unsafe | Safer |
| --- | --- |
| "You are Tier A." | "Your restaurant could be a strong fit for a partnership conversation." |
| "You are a Dream Brand for us." | "Your brand appears aligned with the type of restaurant selection we want to offer customers." |
| "Customers are searching for you." | "There appears to be interest in this type of cuisine or category." |
| "There is Low Choice in your category." | "We are working to broaden the range of relevant restaurant options in your area." |
| "We can increase your orders." | "It may be worth exploring whether the platform could become an additional channel." |
| "We outperform your current platform." | "It may be useful to compare whether an additional channel could complement what you already have." |
| "Your past performance was weak." | "I would like to understand what did or did not work previously." |
| "Commission was your concern before." | "I know the commercial model has to make sense, and I would like to understand your priorities there." |

## Handling Internal Signals

Internal signals may shape strategy but must be translated into external-safe language.

Example:

- Internal: `top_search = true`, search terms include a cuisine category.
- Unsafe: "Customers are searching for this keyword, so you should join."
- Safer: "Your restaurant appears relevant for customers interested in this cuisine or category. It may be worth exploring whether the platform could be an additional channel."

## Handling Historical Transcripts

Do not quote or paraphrase confidential transcript content in merchant-facing copy unless the user explicitly states it is approved for use.

Instead:

- Use internal summaries to prepare the rep.
- Turn sensitive points into discovery questions.
- Flag relationship risks for human review.

## Quality Control Checklist

Before finalizing merchant-facing copy, confirm:

- No internal signal names are present.
- No tier, score, or priority language is present.
- No internal search terms are present.
- No confidential transcript detail is present.
- No performance promise is present.
- No invented fact is present.
- No competitor-negative claim is present.
- No aggressive or mass-outreach language is present.
- Terminated accounts use win-back and listening-first language.
- Human review is explicitly required.
