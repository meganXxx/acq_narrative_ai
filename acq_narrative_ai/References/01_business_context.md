# Business Context

## Purpose

This skill supports restaurant acquisition, reactivation, and win-back outreach for a food delivery platform.

Sales teams contact restaurants to invite them to join the platform, explore partnership opportunities, or restart conversations with previously partnered restaurants.

The skill turns scattered account information into:

1. An Internal Sales Account Brief for Sales decision-making.
2. A Merchant-facing Pitch Narrative that a Sales rep can use or lightly edit after human review.

## Primary Users

- Field Sales representatives preparing outbound calls, visits, emails, or follow-ups.
- Inside Sales representatives qualifying prospects or re-engaging merchants.
- Business Development teams.
- Sales managers reviewing account prioritization and recommended approach.
- Sales enablement teams creating repeatable account research workflows.

## Sales Pain Point

Sales reps often do not have enough time to conduct holistic account research, connect account-specific signals, and convert that research into a tailored, consultative pitch.

The skill helps synthesize:

- Internal account signals.
- Prior relationship history.
- Known objections or pain points.
- Online footprint.
- Sales notes.
- Data quality concerns.
- Pitch coaching best practices.

## Platform Value Proposition

The platform value proposition may include:

- Helping restaurants reach more local customers.
- Providing an additional online ordering channel.
- Increasing visibility among platform users.
- Supporting delivery, pickup, or marketplace discovery.
- Helping restaurants test online demand in a structured way.
- Assisting with onboarding and menu setup.
- Offering consultative partnership conversations rather than hard selling.

These are possible value themes, not guaranteed outcomes.

## Required Output 1: Internal Sales Account Brief

Use this output for Sales decision-making. It may include internal signals, internal prioritization logic, prior interaction history, known objections, termination context, and recommended next actions.

The brief should help a seller decide:

- Whether to prioritize the account.
- What the account context appears to be.
- What risks or sensitivities exist.
- Which pitch angle to test.
- Which discovery questions to ask.
- What evidence supports each recommendation.

## Required Output 2: Merchant-facing Pitch Narrative

Use this output for seller-ready copy or talking points. It must be safe to adapt for a restaurant owner, manager, or operator.

It must not expose internal signals, internal scoring, confidential transcript details, confidential performance data, or unsupported claims. It should frame the conversation around the restaurant's visible strengths, likely goals, and a low-pressure invitation to explore fit.

## Internal vs Merchant-facing Boundary

Internal signals can be used in the Internal Sales Account Brief.

Internal signals must not be exposed in merchant-facing copy.

Merchant-facing copy must not mention:

- Merchant potential tier A/B/C/D.
- Top Search.
- Dream Brand.
- Low Choice.
- Internal search terms.
- Internal scoring or ranking.
- Internal performance data.
- Confidential transcript details.
- Internal account prioritization logic.

The Merchant-facing Pitch Narrative may be informed by internal signals only after translating them into neutral, merchant-safe language grounded in public or merchant-provided facts.

## Outcome Promise Boundary

The skill must not promise guaranteed outcomes such as:

- Order growth.
- Revenue increase.
- Ranking improvement.
- Search placement.
- Customer acquisition.
- Delivery volume.
- ROI or profitability.

If a possible benefit is discussed, use cautious language such as "could", "may", "appears", or "it may be worth exploring."

## Required Handling for Previously Terminated Accounts

Previously terminated, churned, paused, or rejected accounts require win-back and listening-first handling.

For these accounts:

- Do not lead with a generic acquisition pitch.
- Acknowledge internally that prior partnership status may signal sensitivity.
- Use merchant-facing language that invites feedback and explores whether circumstances have changed.
- Do not state or imply a termination reason unless the user supplied it and it is explicitly safe to reference externally.
- Do not pressure the merchant to rejoin.

## Evidence Standard

Every output must include an evidence map and a quality control section.

Each meaningful claim should be traceable to:

- A supplied input field.
- A provided source.
- A clearly labeled inference.
- A missing-data note.

If evidence is weak, recommend discovery questions rather than assertive messaging.

## Human Review Requirement

Human review is required before merchant-facing content is used.

Sales should review merchant-facing drafts for:

- Accuracy.
- Local market appropriateness.
- Brand voice.
- Legal and compliance constraints.
- Relationship sensitivity.
