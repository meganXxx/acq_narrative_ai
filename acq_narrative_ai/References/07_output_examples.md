# Output Examples

All examples are fictional, generic, and not tied to real restaurant data. Merchant-facing examples must not expose internal labels or make performance promises.

These examples use the concise Sales-ready default format. Use detailed mode only when the user asks for a full account brief, full evidence map, or full quality control.

## Top Search + Strong Reviews

# Account: Saffron Harbor Kitchen (sample-top-search)

## 1. Sales Context Summary

- Fictional Indian coastal restaurant in a market district; public signals show a strong rating, high review count, and visible social presence.
- Internal signals suggest strong category interest and high account priority; keep those labels internal.
- No prior conversation, objections, or termination history provided.
- Likely angle: demand-led plus reputation leverage.
- Key caution: do not mention internal search terms or promise orders, revenue, ranking, or customer acquisition.
- Next best action: send a tailored email or call with a discovery-first CTA.

## 2. Pitch Narrative

### Call Opening

Hi, this is [Name] from [Platform]. Your restaurant appears to have strong public visibility around Indian coastal cuisine, and I thought it could be worth a short conversation about whether an additional online channel could fit your goals. Do you have 30 seconds for me to explain?

### Email / Message

Hi [Name],

I am reaching out because your restaurant appears to have a strong public presence around Indian coastal cuisine. It may be worth exploring whether an additional online ordering channel could make it easier for relevant customers to discover you when they are considering delivery or pickup. Rather than assume fit, I would like to understand how you currently think about online ordering and what would need to work operationally for your team. Would you be open to a short conversation?

### Follow-up

Just following up in case a short conversation would be useful. Happy to start with your priorities and see whether there is any fit.

### Objection Handling

- Already on another platform: "That makes sense. I am not assuming you would replace anything; it may be useful to compare whether another channel could complement what you already have."
- Not sure delivery is a fit: "Completely understand. Would it be useful to discuss what would need to be true for delivery or pickup to make sense?"

## 3. Review Notes

- Evidence used: cuisine, location, public rating/review/social signals, internal priority/search signals.
- Do-not-say items: internal search terms, Top Search, tier, guaranteed orders or revenue.
- Missing or uncertain data: decision maker, current delivery setup, merchant goals.
- Hallucination/internal leak risk: low.
- Confidence score: 4; human review required.

## Dream Brand + Operational Concern

# Account: Juniper Table (sample-dream-brand)

## 1. Sales Context Summary

- Fictional modern vegetarian restaurant with strong public footprint and prior interest in online ordering.
- Internal strategy flags indicate high strategic fit; keep the internal label and tier out of merchant-facing copy.
- Known context: prior concerns about kitchen workflow and menu setup workload.
- Likely angle: strategic brand plus operational support.
- Key caution: validate whether operational concerns still apply; do not imply setup is effortless.
- Next best action: call or email with a practical operational-fit conversation.

## 2. Pitch Narrative

### Call Opening

Hi, this is [Name] from [Platform]. Juniper Table appears to have a clear vegetarian concept and strong public presence. I wanted to explore, in a practical way, whether an additional online ordering channel could fit your operations.

### Email / Message

Hi [Name],

I am reaching out because Juniper Table appears to have a clear vegetarian concept and strong public presence. I thought it could be worth a short conversation about whether an additional online ordering channel could support your current priorities. I would not want to assume fit, especially because any setup needs to work operationally. Would it be useful to discuss what menu onboarding and order flow would need to look like for this to make sense?

### Follow-up

Just checking whether a practical conversation would be useful. Happy to focus first on what would need to work operationally.

### Objection Handling

- Delivery could disrupt dine-in service: "That is a fair concern. It would be useful to understand your current order flow before discussing whether any setup could fit."
- Menu setup sounds time-consuming: "I understand. We can keep the conversation focused on what setup would require before deciding whether it is worth pursuing."

## 3. Review Notes

- Evidence used: cuisine, online footprint, prior transcript summary, pain points, known objections, internal strategic signal.
- Do-not-say items: Dream Brand, tier, confidential transcript details, effortless setup claims.
- Missing or uncertain data: whether prior operational concerns remain current.
- Hallucination/internal leak risk: low.
- Confidence score: 4; human review required.

## Previously Terminated / Win-back

# Account: Marigold Noodle House (sample-win-back)

## 1. Sales Context Summary

- Fictional Vietnamese restaurant with prior partnership history and public online footprint.
- Internal signals suggest local opportunity, but termination history is the main context.
- Known context: terminated account; provided reason is high commission concern.
- Likely angle: win-back and listening-first, with commercial-fit discovery.
- Key caution: do not state termination reason externally unless explicitly approved; do not promise improved economics.
- Next best action: call first and ask what would need to be different for a renewed conversation to be useful.

## 2. Pitch Narrative

### Call Opening

Hi, this is [Name] from [Platform]. I understand we may have worked together before, so I do not want to assume a standard conversation is the right starting point. If you are open to it, I would first like to understand what would need to be different for a conversation to be useful now.

### Email / Message

Hi [Name],

I am reaching out because I understand we may have worked together before. I do not want to assume that a standard partnership conversation is the right starting point. If you are open to it, I would first like to listen and understand what would need to be different for a new conversation to be useful. From there, we could explore whether an online ordering conversation fits your current priorities. Would you be open to a short call?

### Follow-up

Just following up gently. If now is not the right time, I understand and would value knowing what would make a future conversation useful.

### Objection Handling

- Commercial model concern: "I understand the commercial model has to make sense. Rather than assume fit, I would like to understand what conditions would need to be in place."
- Tried before and it did not work: "That is completely fair. I would first like to understand what did or did not work from your perspective."

## 3. Review Notes

- Evidence used: prior partnership status, termination status, termination reason, known objection, public footprint.
- Do-not-say items: termination reason externally, prior performance, tier, Low Choice, promised economics.
- Missing or uncertain data: current willingness to reconsider, whether prior concern remains active.
- Hallucination/internal leak risk: medium.
- Confidence score: 4; human review required.

Hi, this is [Name] from [Platform]. I am reaching out to learn how Kofi Corner currently thinks about online ordering, delivery, or pickup. I would not want to assume fit without understanding your priorities.

Just following up in case a short exploratory conversation would be useful. Happy to keep it focused on whether there is any fit.

## 3. Review Notes

- Evidence used: restaurant name, cuisine/category if supplied, data quality notes.
- Do-not-say items: demand claims, public reputation claims, assumed pain points.
- Missing or uncertain data: location, online footprint, decision maker, delivery status, objections.
