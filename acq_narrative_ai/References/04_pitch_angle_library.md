# Pitch Angle Library

Use this library to select a seller-ready narrative angle. Choose angles based on verified data, not generic persuasion. Combine at most two primary angles. Merchant-facing copy must never reveal internal classification signals; instead, translate them into safe, consultative statements grounded in public facts.

## Signal-to-Angle Mapping Matrix

| Internal Signal / Context | Recommended Pitch Angle | Strategic Sales Logic | Merchant-Safe Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Top Search** | Demand-led Pitch | Existing customer search behavior indicates a potential localized appetite for the cuisine type. | "There appears to be consistent customer interest in this category within the local area..." |
| **Dream Brand** | Strategic Brand Pitch | High public brand equity aligns with the platform's focus on curated, premium selections. | "Your brand appears well aligned with the type of quality dining options we prioritize for our users..." |
| **Low Choice** | Local Opportunity Pitch | Marketplace data shows a supply gap for this specific cuisine type in the restaurant's immediate geography. | "We are working to provide customers in your neighborhood with a broader selection of local options..." |
| **Competitor Platform Presence** | Incremental Channel Pitch | Active presence on other networks proves delivery operational literacy and digital channel acceptance. | "If you are already leveraging online channels, it may be worth exploring how a complementary channel fits your goals..." |
| **Previously Terminated** | Win-back Pitch | Re-establishing contact requires a feedback-driven loop to address historical operational or commercial friction. | "I understand we have partnered in the past. If you are open to it, I would love to understand what would need to be different..." |
| **Operational Concerns** | Operational Support Pitch | High internal friction points (e.g., prep times, staff issues) require a high-touch, controlled logistics setup. | "We can keep our focus entirely on practical logistics, including customized prep windows and menu optimization..." |
| **Strong Online Footprint** | Reputation Leverage Pitch | High ratings (Google/Instagram) indicate a proven product-market fit that can be transferred to a digital channel. | "Your restaurant has built great public visibility, and it could be worth exploring how to extend that to digital delivery users..." |

## Pitch Angle Application & Rewrite Specifications

### 1. Demand-led Pitch
* **Best For:** Top Search flags, high organic cuisine searches, high local density populations.
* **Tactical Execution:** Position the platform as a tool to capture existing, unfulfilled local interest. Focus on discovery rather than guaranteed conversion.
* **Strict Avoidance:** Never name specific internal search volumes, exact keywords, or promise incoming order loops.
* **Rewrite Panel:**
    * *Before (Revealing Internal Signal - VIOLATION):* "You are marked as a Top Search brand on our app, meaning hundreds of people are typing your name in. If you sign up today, your orders will skyrocket."
    * *After (Merchant-Safe & Consultative - COMPLIANT):* "Based on general demand trends in your neighborhood, there appears to be clear interest in high-quality options within your category. It could be worth evaluating whether an additional digital presence would make it easier for those local customers to discover your menu."

### 2. Strategic Brand Pitch
* **Best For:** High reputation scores, iconic local footprints, premium culinary concepts.
* **Tactical Execution:** Frame the outreach as an invitation to a curated network. Treat the merchant as an anchor partner with high operational respect.
* **Strict Avoidance:** Avoid excessive flattery or making them feel the platform is desperate for their presence. Do not reveal internal high-tier status.
* **Rewrite Panel:**
    * *Before (Excessive Flattery - VIOLATION):* "You are our absolute Tier A Dream Brand. We need you on the platform to make our app look good, and we will guarantee you the highest placement."
    * *After (Professional & Selected - COMPLIANT):* "Your restaurant has established a distinct reputation for quality in this market. We are focused on ensuring our digital selection reflects the top-tier dining options in the area, and your operational model appears highly aligned with that direction."

### 3. Local Opportunity Pitch
* **Best For:** Low Choice parameters, areas lacking specific culinary variety, geographic expansion lines.
* **Tactical Execution:** Highlight neighborhood diversification. Frame the restaurant as an essential addition that fills a distinct local gap.
* **Strict Avoidance:** Never state that the area is "undersupplied" due to platform failure, or that the marketplace is currently struggling for selection.
* **Rewrite Panel:**
    * *Before (Negative Supply Gaps - VIOLATION):* "We don't have any options in your zip code right now and our users are complaining. You will face zero competition if you join."
    * *After (Geographic Alignment - COMPLIANT):* "We are actively optimizing our platform configuration to bring local neighborhood users a more balanced selection of regional cuisines. Given your location and focus, your kitchen could be a great fit for expanding that local access."

### 4. Incremental Channel Pitch
* **Best For:** Restaurants visibly running competitor tablets, active multi-platform setups.
* **Tactical Execution:** Position the platform as a low-risk, multi-homing strategy designed to diversify risk and access unique customer cohorts.
* **Strict Avoidance:** Never speak negatively about competitor platforms, criticize their fees, or claim superior performance metrics without verifiable data.
* **Rewrite Panel:**
    * *Before (Competitor Attacking - VIOLATION):* "I see you use Competitor X. Their delivery times are terrible and their fees eat your profits. Switch to us for better revenue."
    * *After (Incremental Channel Value - COMPLIANT):* "Many operational setups successfully utilize multiple digital channels to reach different customer segments. Rather than replacing your current systems, it may be useful to see if our local audience could serve as a productive, secondary channel to balance your kitchen's weekly capacity."

### 5. Win-back Pitch
* **Best For:** Churned, paused, historically frustrated, or previously terminated accounts.
* **Tactical Execution:** Lead with radical operational listening. Validate past friction points and present specific structural, software, or policy changes made since their departure.
* **Strict Avoidance:** Do not push onboarding links immediately. Never imply the merchant was responsible for past failures.
* **Rewrite Panel:**
    * *Before (Aggressive Acquisition - VIOLATION):* "I know you quit three years ago because of bad orders, but we have fixed everything now. Sign this new contract to start getting orders again."
    * *After (Listening-First Re-engagement - COMPLIANT):* "I recognize that we have partnered previously, and I appreciate that the experience may not have aligned with your operational requirements at that time. If you are open to it, I would value the opportunity to listen to what went wrong, and see if our updated operational controls make a conversation worth revisiting."

### 6. Operational Support Pitch
* **Best For:** Accounts with explicitly documented workflow constraints, high prep times, or understaffing notes.
* **Tactical Execution:** Frame the platform around precision settings—such as order throttling, custom delivery radii, and flexible tablet settings that hand control back to the kitchen staff.
* **Strict Avoidance:** Do not claim that online operations are completely effortless or solve internal labor shortages.
* **Rewrite Panel:**
    * *Before (Over-promising Simplification - VIOLATION):* "Don't worry about being understaffed; our tablet handles everything automatically and won't add any extra stress to your team."
    * *After (Controlled Operations - COMPLIANT):* "We recognize that maintaining pace in the kitchen is critical to your brand. Our workflow architecture allows teams to adjust preparation parameters, throttle order volumes, or pause incoming streams during busy dine-in shifts to ensure your internal operations are never overwhelmed."

### 7. Reputation Leverage Pitch
* **Best For:** Verified high review volumes, deep digital engagement across social media channels.
* **Tactical Execution:** Connect their established, real-world community goodwill to a digital distribution system that unlocks access for off-site dining occasions.
* **Strict Avoidance:** Do not infer exact conversion math or claim that digital followers automatically translate into guaranteed order volumes.
* **Rewrite Panel:**
    * *Before (Follower-to-Order Guarantees - VIOLATION):* "You have 20k Instagram followers, so if you join our app, we will instantly convert those fans into thousands of orders."
    * *After (Reputation Transition - COMPLIANT):* "Your concept has built an impressive level of public engagement and positive customer feedback. It could be valuable to explore how to make your menu more accessible to those advocates during dining occasions when they are unable to visit your physical location."
