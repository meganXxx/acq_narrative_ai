# Pitch Angle Library

Use this library to select a seller-ready narrative angle. Choose angles based on evidence, not generic persuasion. Combine at most two primary angles unless the user asks for a longer strategy.

Merchant-facing copy must not reveal internal signals. Translate internal reasons into safe, consultative language.

## Signal-to-Angle Mapping

| Signal or Context | Recommended Pitch Angle |
| --- | --- |
| Top Search | Demand-led Pitch |
| Dream Brand | Strategic Brand Pitch |
| Low Choice | Local Opportunity Pitch |
| Competitor Platform Presence | Incremental Channel Pitch |
| Previously Terminated | Win-back Pitch |
| Operational concerns | Operational Support Pitch |
| Strong Google rating, review count, Instagram following, or public brand presence | Reputation Leverage Pitch |

## Demand-led Pitch

Best for:

- Top Search accounts.
- Strong cuisine or category interest.
- Search terms indicating user interest.

Sales logic:

There may be existing customer interest in this type of restaurant or cuisine.

Merchant-safe narrative:

"There appears to be customer interest in this type of cuisine or category, and it may be worth exploring whether the platform could make it easier for relevant customers to discover you."

Use when:

- Search signals are strong.
- Account is in a relevant category.
- The restaurant is not previously terminated for unresolved performance reasons.

Avoid:

- Promising orders.
- Revealing search terms without approval.
- Stating internal demand data too directly.

## Strategic Brand Pitch

Best for:

- Dream Brand accounts.
- Strong brand recognition.
- Strong public reputation.
- High potential tier accounts.

Sales logic:

The restaurant may add strategic value to the platform's selection.

Merchant-safe narrative:

"Your brand appears well aligned with the kind of quality restaurant selection we want to offer customers."

Use when:

- Brand perception matters.
- Account is high priority.
- Sales should take a respectful, high-touch approach.

Avoid:

- Revealing the Dream Brand label.
- Over-flattering.
- Sounding like generic praise.

## Local Opportunity Pitch

Best for:

- Low Choice accounts.
- Supply gap areas.
- Underrepresented cuisine and location combinations.

Sales logic:

The platform may benefit from adding more relevant supply in the area.

Merchant-safe narrative:

"We are working to bring customers in your area a broader range of relevant restaurant options, and your restaurant could be a strong fit for that direction."

Use when:

- Location is important.
- Cuisine is relevant to local demand.
- Restaurant has enough quality signals.

Avoid:

- Saying the area is under-supplied.
- Mentioning internal marketplace gaps.
- Suggesting the restaurant is only valuable because supply is weak.

## Incremental Channel Pitch

Best for:

- Restaurants already on competitor platforms.
- Restaurants with delivery experience.
- Restaurants with a strong online footprint.

Sales logic:

The merchant may be open to additional online channels.

Merchant-safe narrative:

"If you are already using online ordering channels, it may be worth exploring whether our platform could complement what you already have."

Use when:

- Competitor presence is visible.
- Merchant likely understands online ordering.
- Sales wants to position the platform as an additional channel.

Avoid:

- Criticizing competitors.
- Claiming better performance.
- Suggesting the restaurant should replace existing platforms.

## Win-back Pitch

Best for:

- Previously partnered restaurants.
- Terminated, churned, paused, or rejected accounts.
- Accounts with historical context or known objections.

Sales logic:

The relationship may be restarted if past concerns are understood and the merchant is open to revisiting fit.

Merchant-safe narrative:

"I understand we may have worked together before. If you are open to it, I would like to understand what would need to be different for a conversation to be useful now."

Use when:

- Prior partnership exists.
- Termination reason is known or needs validation.
- Sales should avoid pushing too fast.

Avoid:

- Stating internal termination details externally.
- Assuming the issue is resolved.
- Blaming the merchant.
- Overpromising fixes.

## Operational Support Pitch

Best for:

- Accounts with operational complexity concerns.
- Merchants worried about delivery setup, menu management, onboarding, kitchen workflow, or order handling.

Sales logic:

The pitch should reduce perceived operational uncertainty through practical, consultative discussion.

Merchant-safe narrative:

"We can keep the conversation practical and focus on what the setup would require, including menu onboarding and operational fit."

Use when:

- Transcript or notes mention operational concerns.
- Merchant may be cautious about delivery complexity.
- Termination reason or objections relate to operations.

Avoid:

- Claiming setup is effortless.
- Ignoring merchant constraints.
- Saying "we can solve everything."

## Reputation Leverage Pitch

Best for:

- Strong Google rating.
- High review count.
- Strong Instagram following.
- Strong public brand presence.

Sales logic:

The restaurant already has public visibility that may support an online discovery conversation.

Merchant-safe narrative:

"Your restaurant already appears to have strong public visibility, and it may be worth exploring whether an additional online channel could make it easier for customers to discover you."

Use when:

- Public data supports reputation.
- Account has meaningful online footprint.
- Online footprint is current enough to cite.

Avoid:

- Inferring exact demand.
- Claiming strong conversion.
- Overstating follower or review impact.

## Selection Rules

1. If the account is previously terminated, churned, paused, or rejected, lead with Win-back Pitch.
2. If strong known objections exist, pair the selected angle with Operational Support Pitch or a consultative objection-aware approach.
3. If online footprint is strong, consider Reputation Leverage Pitch.
4. If internal strategic signals are strong, use them only to select an angle, then translate externally into merchant-safe language.
5. If data is thin, use a consultative discovery-first pitch rather than asserting a strategic angle.
