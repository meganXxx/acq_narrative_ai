# Pitch Coaching Best Practices

This document acts as the definitive sales methodology layer for all merchant-facing outputs. It provides an operational architecture to transform abstract raw signals into precise, human-sounding, and consultative outreach dialogues.

---

## Core Pitching Principles
Every single communication script, text string, and email narrative must comply with these instructions:
* **Contextual Over Introduction:** Never begin with a generic introduction of the platform. Lead with a verified, merchant-specific public observation.
* **Consultative Neutrality:** Replace aggressive closing lines with low-pressure discovery check-ins. Position the platform as a collaborative partner rather than an outsourced utility.
* **The Guardrail Filter:** Ensure zero internal terminology or absolute outcome promises slip into the copy. Use risk-conscious language (*"may," "could," "explore"*).

---

## Advanced Question Sequencing: SPIN Framework

Do not generate random or unstructured lists of open-ended queries. Sales reps must build a progressive discovery path using this sequential model:
[ Situation Question ] -> Establishes operational baselines.
↓
[  Problem Question  ] -> Uncovers hidden systemic friction points.
↓
[Implication Question] -> Quantifies the long-term cost of inaction.
↓
[ Need-Payoff Question] -> Allows the merchant to articulate value.
### 1. Situation Questions
* **Goal:** Establish factual, non-threatening baseline data regarding current kitchen operations and delivery infrastructure.
* **Introductory Cue:** *"Before diving into details, I want to make sure I understand how your day-to-day operations run right now."*
* **Compliant Scripting:**
    * "What channels are you currently using to connect your menu with off-site customers?"
    * "How are you managing digital order flows alongside your main in-house POS setup?"
    * "What tools or internal tracking systems do you use to evaluate delivery turnaround times?"

### 2. Problem Questions
* **Goal:** Gently guide the merchant to surface specific operational friction points, frustrations, or capacity constraints.
* **Introductory Cue:** *"What are the main operational bottlenecks preventing your kitchen from smoothly handling peak periods?"*
* **Compliant Scripting:**
    * "How do your current off-site channels perform during intense dinner or weekend rushes?"
    * "Are you experiencing any consistent challenges with order accuracy or delivery dispatch coordination?"
    * "What is your biggest operational frustration with your current digital setup?"

### 3. Implication Questions
* **Goal:** Help the merchant recognize the broader business impact, financial strains, or reputation costs of leaving those problems unresolved.
* **Introductory Cue:** *"Let’s look at how these persistent challenges impact the overall health of your business."*
* **Compliant Scripting:**
    * "When dispatch delays occur during peak hours, how does that affect your local customer review profile or repeat business?"
    * "What impact do inconsistent order flows have on your weekly staff scheduling and overhead costs?"
    * "If these logistics bottlenecks aren't resolved this quarter, what does that mean for your kitchen's overall efficiency?"

### 4. Need-Payoff Questions
* **Goal:** Prompt the merchant to articulate the practical value of a working solution, shifting their focus toward a collaborative next step.
* **Introductory Cue:** *"Imagine an operational setup where your delivery logistics run smoothly—how would that affect your workflow?"*
* **Compliant Scripting:**
    * "If you could throttle your digital order intake during unexpected dine-in rushes, how would that protect your kitchen staff's stress levels?"
    * "Would having a dedicated, managed courier network help stabilize your delivery dispatch times?"
    * "How would gaining access to clean, real-time performance metrics change your approach to menu planning?"

## Advanced Objection Handling: The LUPP Model

When a merchant raises an objection, sales reps must follow the **LUPP Model** (Listen $\rightarrow$ Unpack $\rightarrow$ Prescribe $\rightarrow$ Pivot). This approach replaces direct counter-arguments with an empathetic, discovery-driven conversation.
[ LISTEN ] Validate feelings with an empathetic statement.
↓
[ UNPACK ] Ask a targeted discovery question to pinpoint the root cause.
↓
[PRESCRIBE] Introduce a safe, flexible operational capability.
↓
[ PIVOT  ] Ask a low-pressure closing question to advance the talk.
### 1. Competitor Platform Objections

#### Scenario A: "We get more orders on our current competitor platform."
* **Listen:** "I completely understand. A consistent flow of orders is essential for keeping any kitchen running smoothly."
* **Unpack:** "Out of curiosity, are those specific orders helping you maintain your targeted profit margins, or do they require heavy discounting to perform?"
* **Prescribe:** "Our approach focuses on optimizing for balanced, sustainable orders—especially during your kitchen's off-peak windows—using targeted campaigns that protect your base margins."
* **Pivot:** "Would you be open to exploring how a secondary channel could complement your existing setup without compromising your current margins?"

#### Scenario B: "Competitors offer lower commission fees."
* **Listen:** "I completely hear you. When margins are tight, every single percentage point matters to your bottom line."
* **Unpack:** "When evaluating those fee structures, are you looking strictly at the base percentage cost, or are you also tracking the level of marketing support and localized driver care you receive in return?"
* **Prescribe:** "Our commercial structures are built as an all-inclusive investment. Beyond simple courier dispatch, they cover localized marketing placements, active data reporting, and dedicated merchant support lines."
* **Pivot:** "Would it be helpful to run a quick comparative analysis to see if the net return matches your operational goals?"

#### Scenario C: "Competitor delivery speeds are faster because they don't stack orders."
* **Listen:** "I appreciate you sharing that. Food quality and thermal retention at the customer's door are absolutely vital to your brand reputation."
* **Unpack:** "What is your main priority for your off-site business right now—guaranteeing rapid dispatch for every single item, or building an expanded delivery radius to reach more distant neighborhoods?"
* **Prescribe:** "While batched delivery is structurally designed to optimize courier routes and keep customer fees predictable, we offer customizable preparation windows and radius limits that keep your kitchen in total control of food quality."
* **Pivot:** "If we tailored your delivery radius to protect your food's presentation, would that make a trial run worth exploring?"

---

### 2. Financial & Profit Margin Objections

#### Scenario A: "Your platform is simply too expensive; margins are too thin."
* **Listen:** "I completely validate that concern. Rising ingredient costs and fixed overhead leave very little breathing room."
* **Unpack:** "How are you currently calculating the profitability of your digital orders versus your walk-in traffic?"
* **Prescribe:** "Digital delivery is designed to leverage your existing fixed costs (like rent and baseline staff wages) to capture incremental revenue. Because you only pay a fee on completed orders, it can act as an additional layer of support for your bottom line."
* **Pivot:** "If we sat down to review a tailored model that helps protect your core margins, would you be open to evaluating the math?"

#### Scenario B: "I don't want to mark up my menu prices on the app, but I have to in order to make a profit."
* **Listen:** "That is a very fair balancing act. You want to keep your customer pricing uniform while protecting your operating margins."
* **Unpack:** "Have you noticed if price differences across platforms impact how often your regular customers choose to order from you?"
* **Prescribe:** "We generally recommend focusing your digital menu on high-margin signature items or curated multi-item combos. This strategy helps protect your profitability without requiring a broad, high markup across your entire menu."
* **Pivot:** "Would you be open to co-creating a streamlined delivery menu focused exclusively on your most profitable items?"

---

### 3. Operational & Trust Objections

#### Scenario A: "We are already too busy in-house and can't handle extra orders."
* **Listen:** "That is a fantastic position to be in. Strong dine-in traffic is a clear indicator that your product-market fit is highly successful."
* **Unpack:** "Are there specific hours during the week, such as mid-afternoon or late evening, where your kitchen capacity naturally slows down?"
* **Prescribe:** "Our software allows complete operational flexibility. You can configure your tablet to accept orders exclusively during your slower off-peak windows, or completely pause incoming requests during a hectic dine-in rush."
* **Pivot:** "If we set up the system to run only during your quieter hours to fill operational gaps, would that approach fit your current workflow?"

#### Scenario B: "I am worried delivery will cannibalize my regular dine-in customers."
* **Listen:** "That is a very valid concern. You want to make sure you aren't shifting your profitable walk-in traffic over to a delivery screen."
* **Unpack:** "What percentage of your current weekly revenue comes from loyal dine-in regulars versus transient or first-time digital discoverers?"
* **Prescribe:** "Market data shows that digital platform users represent a highly distinct customer cohort. These are often individuals who are browsing from home or offices and would not have physically walked through your doors for that specific dining occasion."
* **Pivot:** "Would you be interested in running a short, targeted trial to see if we draw in a new customer base rather than shifting your current one?"

---

### 4. CPC & Premium Value Objections

#### Scenario A: "How much ad exposure can you explicitly guarantee if I invest in CPC?"
* **Listen:** "I completely understand your focus here. If you are allocating marketing capital, you need to know exactly how your investment is performing."
* **Unpack:** "Are you looking primarily for broad brand visibility and impressions, or are you focused on paying exclusively for customers who take action and click on your menu?"
* **Prescribe:** "Because our digital marketing model runs on a Cost-Per-Click mechanism, we don't offer generic impression guarantees. Instead, you only pay when an interested customer actively selects your restaurant, ensuring your budget is tied directly to clear intent."
* **Pivot:** "Would you like to test a low-risk pilot campaign with a strict daily budget cap to monitor its real-world performance?"

## Multi-Channel Messaging Matrix by Relationship Temperature

Sales reps must match their outreach tone to the merchant's current relationship status. Do not send deep closing pitches to cold accounts, and do not send generic templates to engaged ones.

### 1. Cold Outreach (Unfamiliar Prospect)
* **Strategic Objective:** Build baseline credibility, mention one safe public observation, and secure permission to continue the conversation.
* **Phone Script:**
    "Hi, this is [Name] from [Platform]. I’ll keep this brief as I know your kitchen is busy. I've been following your positive local feedback in [Area] and noticed your focus on [Cuisine type]. We are currently speaking with selected operators in the neighborhood about options for expanding their digital accessibility. Do you have 30 seconds to discuss why I reached out?"
* **Email Content:**
    **Subject:** Balanced digital options for [Restaurant Name]
    "Hi [Manager Name], I came across [Restaurant Name] while researching top-rated restaurants in [Area]. I am reaching out because many local operators look for ways to balance their kitchen capacity during off-peak windows without disrupting their main dine-in floor. We provide customized logistics setups built to handle exactly that. If you are open to it, would you have a few minutes this week for a brief introductory call?"
* **In-Store Approach:**
    "Hi, my name is [Name] from [Platform]. I wanted to drop off a quick contact card for the owner or kitchen manager. We are introducing a set of flexible operational tools designed to help local concepts reach off-site neighborhoods. I see you are currently setting up for the shift, so I won't take your time. Is there a preferred email address where I can send a quick overview?"

### 2. Warm Outreach (Demonstrated Interest)
* **Strategic Objective:** Transition from general awareness to collaborative problem-solving using early **Problem/Implication** questions.
* **Phone Script:**
    "Hi [Manager Name], this is [Name] from [Platform]. We briefly connected last week regarding your digital channels. I’ve been reviewing your local layout, and I wanted to see if you are currently exploring ways to reach customers outside your immediate walking radius, or if your main focus is on streamlining your current pickup counter?"
* **Email Content:**
    **Subject:** Tailoring an online channel for [Restaurant Name]
    "Hi [Manager Name], following up on our brief conversation regarding your online setup. Many restaurants tell us that their biggest concern with adding a new channel is the fear of overwhelming their kitchen staff during dinner rushes. Our platform offers specific order throttling and custom preparation windows to prevent exactly that. Would you be open to a 10-minute conversation this Thursday to review how these controls work?"
* **In-Store Approach:**
    "Hi [Manager Name], great to see you again. When we spoke last week, you mentioned that managing driver handoffs can sometimes create bottlenecks at your front counter. I wanted to show you a quick, two-minute visual of how our localized dispatch system coordinates courier arrivals to keep your entryway clear. Do you have a quick moment to look at this layout?"

### 3. Engaged Outreach (Ready for Final Evaluation)
* **Strategic Objective:** Resolve lingering operational anxieties using **Need-Payoff** questions and clear, actionable onboarding paths.
* **Phone Script:**
    "Hi [Manager Name], it's [Name] from [Platform]. We've aligned on your menu structure and margin model. I’m calling to see if your team has any remaining operational questions regarding the tablet integration, or if you are ready to set your preferred launch date for next week?"
* **Email Content:**
    **Subject:** Onboarding roadmap for [Restaurant Name]
    "Hi [Manager Name], I appreciate your time over the past few weeks. We have configured your custom delivery zone to protect food quality, and mapped out a high-margin menu selection that safeguards your profitability. The final step is a seamless activation process that includes complete menu build-out and live team training. Which afternoon this week works best to drop off your device and run through a quick walk-through with your staff?"
* **In-Store Approach:**
    "Hi [Manager Name], I have the finalized launch package right here, built exactly around the custom parameters we discussed—including your limited off-peak hours and your specific signature dishes. I want to make sure your front-of-house team feels completely confident using the interface. Let's unbox the device together right now and run a quick test order so everyone can see how straightforward the process is."

## Final Pitch Quality Checklist & Failure Modes

Before any merchant-facing copy is finalized or presented to a sales rep, the AI agent must run this automated self-audit. If a single violation is flagged, the output must be regenerated.

* [ ] **The Language Filter:** Does the text contain internal terms like *Dream Brand, Top Search, Low Choice,* or reference ranking tiers? (Must be false).
* [ ] **The Promise Check:** Does the pitch state or imply any absolute guarantees regarding *order volume, revenue growth, ranking position, or customer acquisition metrics*? (Must be false).
* [ ] **The Framework Audit:** Does the objection handling path strictly follow the sequential **LUPP steps** (Listen $\rightarrow$ Unpack $\rightarrow$ Prescribe $\rightarrow$ Pivot)? (Must be true).
* [ ] **The Tone Verification:** Does the dialogue sound conversational, professional, and easily readable by a human agent? (Must be true).

### Top Critical Failure Modes to Avoid
* **Failure Mode 1 (The False Savior Pattern):** Writing copy that positions the platform as the ultimate solution to complex internal business issues (e.g., *"We will fix your bad reviews and solve your staffing shortages"*). *Correction:* Keep phrases focused on practical operational collaboration (*"Our tools can provide additional options to help manage peak delivery handoffs"*).
* **Failure Mode 2 (The Data Over-Share):** Translating a "Top Search" flag into a direct data statement (e.g., *"We noticed you are the most searched restaurant in this zip code on our platform"*). *Correction:* Keep data generalized and anchored to the public domain (*"Based on general local cuisine demand trends in this neighborhood..."*).
* **Failure Mode 3 (The Accelerated Close):** Pitching an onboarding link or contract signing step to an account categorized under a Cold relationship temperature state. *Correction:* Enforce a strict boundary that forces cold outreach to focus entirely on curiosity and permission checks.
