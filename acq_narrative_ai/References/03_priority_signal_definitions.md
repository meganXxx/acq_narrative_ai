# Priority Signal Definitions

Priority signals help Sales decide why an account may deserve attention. They are internal decision aids, not merchant-facing talking points.

Merchant-facing examples must not reveal internal labels such as Top Search, Dream Brand, Low Choice, A/B/C/D tier, internal search data, internal ranking, or internal scoring.

## Top Search

Definition:

The restaurant, cuisine, or related category is linked to strong internal user search demand.

Internal Sales meaning:

There may be existing user interest that makes this account relevant for acquisition or reactivation.

Recommended strategy:

Demand-led Pitch.

Merchant-safe phrasing:

"There appears to be interest in this type of cuisine or category, and it may be worth exploring whether the platform could make it easier for relevant customers to discover you."

Do not say:

- "You are a Top Search target."
- "Our internal search data shows users are searching for you."
- "You will get more orders because customers are searching for this."
- "These are the search terms we see for your category."

## Dream Brand

Definition:

The restaurant is considered an internal strategic brand target.

Internal Sales meaning:

The account may have brand value, visibility, selection quality, or strategic importance beyond short-term performance.

Recommended strategy:

Strategic Brand Pitch.

Merchant-safe phrasing:

"Your brand appears well aligned with the kind of quality restaurant selection we want to offer customers."

Do not say:

- "You are one of our Dream Brands."
- "You are on our strategic target list."
- "Our leadership wants this account."
- "You are a priority target in our internal system."

## Low Choice

Definition:

The account is located in an area or category where the platform has limited merchant supply relative to user interest.

Internal Sales meaning:

This merchant could help improve local selection.

Recommended strategy:

Local Opportunity Pitch.

Merchant-safe phrasing:

"We are currently working to bring customers in your area a broader range of relevant restaurant options."

Do not say:

- "Our internal marketplace data shows this area is under-supplied."
- "You are needed because we lack supply here."
- "Your category has a low-choice score."

## Merchant Potential Tier

Definition:

Internal classification of expected commercial or strategic potential, such as A/B/C/D.

Internal Sales meaning:

Helps prioritize Sales effort, outreach depth, and manager review.

Recommended strategy:

Higher potential accounts may receive more customized, higher-touch outreach. Lower potential accounts may receive lighter discovery unless other signals are strong.

Merchant-safe phrasing:

"We believe your restaurant could be a strong fit for a partnership conversation."

Do not say:

- "You are an A-tier merchant."
- "We ranked you highly in our internal scoring."
- "You are a low-tier account."

## Top GTC or Competitor Platform Presence

Definition:

The restaurant appears on one or more competing delivery or ordering platforms.

Internal Sales meaning:

The restaurant may already understand marketplace operations, delivery workflows, and online ordering tradeoffs.

Recommended strategy:

Incremental Channel Pitch.

Merchant-safe phrasing:

"If you are already using online ordering channels, it may be worth exploring whether our platform could complement what you already have."

Do not say:

- "We can outperform your current platform."
- "You should leave your current platform."
- "Your competitor platform is worse."
- "Your competitors are ahead."

## Previously Terminated

Definition:

The restaurant previously partnered with the platform and later terminated, churned, paused, or was delisted.

Internal Sales meaning:

Sales should understand why the prior relationship ended before pitching again. The account requires relationship-sensitive, listening-first handling.

Recommended strategy:

Win-back Pitch.

Merchant-safe phrasing:

"I understand we may have spoken or worked together before. If you are open to it, I would like to understand what would need to be different for a conversation to be useful now."

Do not say:

- "You terminated because of [internal reason], but we can fix it."
- "You failed on the platform before."
- "Your previous performance was weak."
- "We know why you left."

## Priority Synthesis Order

When multiple signals exist, synthesize them in this order:

1. Relationship sensitivity: prior rejection, churn, termination, or unresolved objections.
2. Commercial or strategic priority: potential tier and priority signals.
3. Merchant relevance: cuisine, location, online footprint, visible strengths.
4. Objection readiness: prior concerns and likely friction.
5. Data confidence: missing, stale, or conflicting evidence.
